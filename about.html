About Dr. Kuo

Dr. Karen Kuo is a board-certified internist and wound care specialist with fifteen years of clinical experience. She actively educates and advises students who aspire to become physicians, physician assistants, dentists, and nurses. Their testimonials can be found at www.facebook.com/kuorageousMDs.
Her elite academic training has opened a world of career opportunities in medicine and beyond. Her additional graduate school training (NYU JD class of 2016) attests to her understanding of how admissions process operates. She provides candid, firsthand insight into school (college, medical, law) admissions process, doctoring, American healthcare system, risk management, career options for physicians, and more. Let her guide you in your higher pursuit!

Education

  • City College of New York-Sophie Davis School of Biomedical Education (Admissions Committee Student Interviewer)
  • New York University School of Medicine, on a full merit scholarship by the National Health Service Corps
  • NYU-Tisch, Bellevue, and Manhattan Veterans Administration Hospital (categorical internal medicine internship)
  • NYU-North Shore University Hospital (internal medicine residency)
  • New York University School of Law

Professional Experience

  • Lieutenant Commander, United States Public Health Services, Dept of Homeland Security
  • Clinical Director, Division of Immigration Health Services
  • Concierge medicine, private practice in Manhattan, NY
  • Outpatient internal medicine, private practice in New York City and Long Island
  • Expert witness for medical malpractice case

What is Unique About my Services

  • Personal mentor for entire admissions process; individualized, customized one on one attention for the entire application process from beginning to end, and beyond
    • Some students have forged lifetime friendships with me and are now physicians looking for advice on how to gain entry into residency programs
  • Network of premedical students, medical students, residents in training, and practicing attending physicians
  • Clinical volunteer opportunities and possible research opportunities
  • Firsthand experience in admissions process, in being a medical student, in being an active practicing physician, and in becoming an older non- traditional graduate student
  • If you happen to volunteer for me or my physician colleagues, our letter of recommendation arises from intimate knowledge of you as a total person, rather than just a student volunteer lost in a sea of faces at a large medical institution.
  • Offer ideas on scholarship opportunities (and other advice not provided by college guidance counselors)

The Wall of Success: Role Models

Kuorageous’ proud network of successful elites adds real world insight and serves as invaluable resource for our students. The access to working professionals of varied industries affords unique opportunities and practical wisdom beyond that typically provided by an advisor whose experience is rooted solely in the admissions process. Whatever your interest, we will attempt to find a real working professional in that industry willing to offer “big sister/brother” insight.

Alessandro Caraa, ESQUIRE

Mr. Carra graduated with cum laude honors from Bocconi University in Milan, Italy for both his Bachelor of Law and his Master of Science in Law. He also studied at the University of Texas School of Law in Austin, University of Babson College in Boston, and earned a LLM from NYU School of Law. Currently, he is an Associate at a major law firm in Italy, Gianni-Origoni , Grippo-Cappelli Partners.


Catherine Owens, Esquire

Catherine Owens graduated Magna Cum Laude from Bryn Mawr College with honors in mathematics. She served as the teacher’s assistant for Calculus II, published an original theorem in Integers: Electronic Journal of Combinatorial Number Theory, and won the Anna Pell Wheeler Award for Excellence in Mathematics. Ms. Owens earned her law degree from NYU Law School, where she also served as the editor of a law journal in law and politics and a mock trial teacher for high school students interested in the law. Ms. Owens has worked in the Criminal Division at the United States Attorney's Office for the Eastern District of New York and also at the Liberty and National Security program (Brennan Center). She now works as a litigation lawyer at WilmerHale.


Darien K. Smith, JD

Darien earned his BBA in finance with a minor in mathematics from the University of Miami and his JD from NYU Law. Having worked in Goldman Sachs, Stroock & Stroock & Lavan, and currently at a major New York law firm, Mr. Smith possesses a wealth of experience in international law and business, mergers and acquisitions, and economics.


Diana Hyun, JD

In 2011, Ms. Hyun graduated from Cornell University with a B.S. in Industrial and Labor Relations. Ms. Hyun then received a J.D. from New York University School of Law in 2016, where she served as the Articles Editor for the Annual Survey of American Law and as Alumni Chair for the Asian-Pacific American Law Students Association. Errol Gordon, MD

Eva Chmielnicki, PhD

Eva Chmielnicki is the Executive Director of the Institute for Computational Medicine at NYU School of Medicine. Most recently, she was an Academic Research Manager at Roche Pharmaceutical Company, where she identified, orchestrated, and managed scientific collaborations between Roche scientists and academic researchers.
